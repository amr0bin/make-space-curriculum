*Not an instruction. An invitation — from a peer, not a teacher. The structure: what you are doing, what you are not looking for, the open door.*

[3–4 sentences in first person. Describe your own starting point for this week's making — not the "right" way to begin, not a prescription, but where you are actually landing. ### The activity — starting points, not prescriptions

*These are suggestions for someone who wants more structure. They are a starting point, not a prescription. Every body and every space is different. Adapt freely.*

**Before you touch anything:** Notice the weight of your body. Notice your breath. Name one thing your body is carrying right now. This is the somatic check-in — it grounds the creative act in physical reality before any emotional exploration begins.

**What you need:** Watercolour paper (or any absorbent surface that won't buckle), a small set of watercolours or whatever fluid pigments are available, water, a brush, somewhere to be undisturbed for twenty to forty minutes.

**One way to begin:**

[3–5 short, plain-language suggestions — not numbered steps toward an outcome, but entry points. Each one names a gesture, not an image. What is the hand invited to do? Pool. Hold. Spread slowly. Let a colour stay longer than feels comfortable. Name the moment of resistance — when the watercolour does something the maker didn't plan — and what to do with it: stay, watch, let it be. End with the second somatic check-in: what has shifted? What does the body know now that it didn't at the start?]

*Time: 20–40 minutes, or as long as the body wants to stay.*
