Other Bodies · A companion to the series map

Companions

The feeling that may arise in each of the fifty-two weeks of Make Space, and the natural body that keeps it company — a site of wisdom, not a symbol.

Feelings are named here in pencil, not ink. They are not a fixed set to be identified and filed, but something discovered by attunement — by staying in the week long enough to feel what is actually there. What is offered is a guess held loosely, and a companion to hold it with.

Some companions return across the year in a different light — a body that teaches one thing in spring can teach another in the dark. That is not repetition. It is a relationship deepening.

Movement One · Weeks 1–13

Foundations

Establishing the field, and the questions worth asking. The feelings here are mostly the feelings of beginning.

Week 1

The Permission to Fail

may arisethe held breath of not good enough

companionthe seedling in darkness

A seedling spends itself upward in the dark, on the strength of the seed alone, before there is any proof it is growing. What it knows: growing and the evidence of growing are not the same absence — and nothing green is possible without this blind, unwitnessed first reach.

Week 2

Somatic Arrival

may arisethe suspicion you have not really arrived yet

companionthe morning tide

The tide comes in before anyone on the shore decides it should, pulled by a moon it cannot see. What it knows: you do not have to manufacture your own presence; you are already, partly, here, answering pulls you did not author.

Week 3

The Architecture of the Studio

may arisethe wish for somewhere safe enough to begin

companionthe nest

A nest is not a place but a practice of returning to the same branch with whatever the day provided. What it knows: safety is built by coming back, not by getting it right the first time.

Week 4

Material Agency

may arisethe small panic of not being in control

companionwater

Water finds the lowest point and does not argue with the ground. What it knows: yielding to what the material wants is not the same as losing — the bleed and the pool were never yours to prevent.

Week 5

Intentionality Without Outcome

may arisethe unease of moving without a destination

companionthe river

A river has a direction without a destination it is hurrying toward. What it knows: you can aim toward a feeling without naming what you will make, and direction alone is enough to move by.

Week 6

The Other Body as Companion

may arisethe unfamiliar ease of being accompanied, not answered

companionlichen

Lichen is two lives grown into one, neither dissolving into the other. What it knows: company is not the same as merger — a presence can stay beside you without trying to resolve you.

Week 7

Defining Creative Health

may arisethe discomfort of belonging to no clear category

companionthe watershed

A watershed belongs to no single stream; it is the gathering that makes all of them possible. What it knows: working in an unnamed space is not being lost — it is holding many sources at once.

Week 8

The Question of Stakeholders

may arisethe unease of noticing who is left out

companionthe commons

A commons is land held together, and easily enclosed by the few. What it knows: every practice was shaped for some bodies and not others, and noticing whose is the beginning of honesty, not the end of it.

Week 9

Creative Access

may arisethe quiet grief of a door that was never opened to you

companiongroundwater

Groundwater lies beneath every surface, reachable only where someone has dug. What it knows: there is a difference between being allowed in and being invited — and the water is no less present for going unreached.

Week 10

The Ethics of Witnessing

may arisethe exposure of watching yourself

companionthe owl

The owl sees completely and does not intervene. What it knows: it is possible to witness yourself without correcting — to see the one who is making without seizing the brush from them.

Week 11

The 3D Emotion Space

may arisethe small vertigo of more than one dimension

companionthe atmosphere

Weather is three conditions at once — temperature, pressure, humidity — never a single thing. What it knows: your feeling was never one feeling, and the extra axes are not a complication to fix but a truth you were not given language for.

Week 12

Differentiated Making

may arisethe worry that changing the practice will dilute it

companionmycelium

Mycelium is one network that fruits differently depending on what the soil requires. What it knows: the network holds while the fruit changes — adaptation is not dilution, and something irreducible survives every version.

Week 13

Collective Empowerment

may arisethe pull between making alone and moving with others

companionthe murmuration

A murmuration has no leader; its coherence is each bird attending to the few nearest it. What it knows: you can be part of a shape larger than yourself without disappearing into it.

Movement Two · Weeks 14–26

Literature

Surveying what research, history, and theory can say. The feelings here are the feelings of encountering knowledge — and its limits.

Week 14

Historical Context

may arisethe humility of arriving late to ancient work

companionthe painted cave

In the cave, art was made before any other building existed. What it knows: this is old work, older than medicine — you are not its inventor but its inheritor.

Week 15

The Neuroscientific Perspective

may arisethe mixed comfort of seeing yourself explained

companionthe myelin sheath

Myelin is insulation laid down by repetition, making each signal faster than the last. What it knows: being explained by the brain can hold you and slightly reduce you at once — both are true, and neither is the whole of what happened.

Week 16

Psychological Impact

may arisethe tentative widening when the body stops bracing

companionthe field in spring

A field expands in spring because the threat of winter has passed; nothing has to be achieved. What it knows: range returns on its own once survival is no longer the only task.

Week 17

Physiological Resilience

may arisea quiet, strange trust in a body you cannot feel working

companionthe immune system

The immune system is everywhere and located nowhere, adapting without being told. What it knows: much of what keeps you is distributed, wordless, and entirely uninterested in your attention.

Week 18

Social Connection Theory

may arisethe ache between being near others and belonging to them

companionthe coral reef

A reef sustains more life than any single organism could, by sheer proximity. What it knows: nearness is not yet belonging — and the distance between them is real, crossable, and not guaranteed.

Week 19

The State of Flow

may arisethe longing for ease, and the honesty about who cannot reach it

companionthe river in full current

A river in full current is not forcing; it moves at the pace the land allows. What it knows: ease like this assumes a security not every nervous system has — and to pretend otherwise is to abandon the people who most need another door.

Week 20

Narrative and Meaning-Making

may arisethe pressure of something unnamed asking to be told

companionthe salmon

The salmon carries the memory of its origin in its body; the return is encoded, not chosen. What it knows: some things ask to be told, and some cannot be made to mean — and telling does not always resolve what it names.

Week 21

Creative Health in Education

may arisea longing for the self before self-consciousness arrived

companionthe forest school

A forest school learns outside the building designed for learning. What it knows: there was a time you made things without watching yourself make them, and the watching was taught — which means it can be set down.

Week 22

Clinical Environments

may arisethe stubbornness of making in a place that doesn't invite it

companionlichen on stone

Lichen finds purchase on bare rock that offers it nothing. What it knows: you do not always get hospitable ground — and life that waits for better conditions sometimes waits forever.

Week 23

Social Prescribing

may arisearriving at a threshold still carrying everything you came with

companionthe estuary

An estuary is the meeting of river and sea, with a chemistry neither makes alone. What it knows: you can be sent somewhere and arrive carrying all of it — the referral changes the water without emptying it.

Week 24

The Depression Treatment Gap

may arisethe heavy knowledge of unreachable need

companionthe aquifer

An aquifer is real water, present and unreachable without the infrastructure to lift it. What it knows: most who need this will never reach it — and the work is to refuse to let that knowledge harden into numbness.

Week 25

Task-Shifting

may arisethe question of what only you can give

companionthe fig wasp

The fig and its wasp are a relationship so specific that neither survives without the other. What it knows: some of a practice can be handed on, and some is irreplaceable — and telling the two apart is the whole of teaching it safely.

Week 26

The Global Shift

may arisethe unfamiliar steadiness of working from enough

companionthe mycorrhizal network

The mycorrhizal network sustains the forest underground, long before any distress shows above. What it knows: the deepest care is preventive and unseen — a deposit made before it is needed, by something that will not be thanked.

Movement Three · Weeks 27–48

Investigation — Other Bodies

The case study: one feeling, one natural body, one act of making, each week. Here the companion does not console — it reframes.

Constellation One — Threshold States

Emotions that stand at the edge between what was and what might be.

Week 27

Octopus / Shame

may ariseshame, as exquisite sensitivity to being seen

companionthe octopus

The octopus changes colour in response to everything it sees; it does not hide, it responds. What it reframes: shame is not a verdict on you — it is a sign you are paying attention.

Week 28

Cave / Fear

may arisefear, as the body preparing

companionthe cave

The cave holds darkness as protection; what waits inside is not threat but accumulation. What it reframes: fear is not only something to escape — it is somewhere to gather.

Week 29

Permafrost / Numbness

may arisenumbness, as a kind of keeping

companionpermafrost

Permafrost holds millennia in suspension — not dead, not lost, waiting for conditions that do not yet exist. What it reframes: numbness is not emptiness; it is storage, and what is stored is not gone.

Week 30

Pine Cone / Guardedness

may ariseguardedness, as discernment about when to open

companionthe pine cone

The pine cone releases its seeds only in heat, waiting for the conditions that make germination possible. What it reframes: guardedness is not armour — it is wisdom about timing.

Constellation Two — Grief and Loss

Emotions that ask the body to carry what cannot be fixed.

Week 31

Moss / Grief

may arisegrief, as a slow, spreading form of love

companionmoss

Moss grows over what is broken, making it bearable without restoring it; it accompanies rather than repairs. What it reframes: grief is not a disorder to be cured — it is patience the colour of velvet.

Week 32

Rain / Sadness

may arisesadness, as honest precipitation

companionrain

Rain disturbs the still surface and does not apologise; the ripples are accurate. What it reframes: sadness is not an interruption of a clear day — it is what honest disturbance looks like.

Week 33

River Delta / Exhaustion

may ariseexhaustion, as the end of a long carrying

companionthe river delta

The delta does not collapse; it spreads slowly into many channels, setting down everything it carried. What it reframes: exhaustion is not defeat — it is the quiet arrival of something carried a long way.

Constellation Three — Activation and Urgency

Emotions that move the body before the mind has decided.

Week 34

Dormant Volcano / Despair

may arisedespair, as energy held at depth

companionthe dormant volcano

The dormant volcano is not empty but pressurised; the quiet surface is not the whole story. What it reframes: despair is not the end — it is power waiting for the conditions that will move it.

Week 35

Deep Ocean / Loneliness

may ariseloneliness, as depth that has not been reached

companionthe deep ocean

The deep ocean is full of life adapted to exist without light or witness, and it makes its own. What it reframes: loneliness is not the absence of connection — it is what it looks like to be generative in the dark.

Week 36

Starling / Anxiety

may ariseanxiety, as extraordinary attunement

companionthe starling

The murmuration moves without a leader because every bird is giving complete attention to the nearest few. What it reframes: anxiety is not fragmentation — it is keeping-close, seen from the outside.

Week 37

Salmon / Urgency

may ariseurgency, as the body following what it knows

companionthe salmon

The salmon does not rush; it moves at exactly the pace the current and the body together require. What it reframes: urgency is not panic — it is a deep orientation, obeyed.

Constellation Four — Joy and Its Complications

Emotions that are not simple, even when they feel good.

Week 38

Firefly / Joy

may arisejoy, as a signal rather than a reward

companionthe firefly

The firefly's light is how it finds its kind — communication, not performance. What it reframes: joy is not a luxury you earn — it is how the body locates belonging.

Week 39

Hummingbird / Delight

may arisedelight, as precision at high speed

companionthe hummingbird

The hummingbird hovers exactly as long as the flower requires — total presence, then gone. What it reframes: delight is not a distraction from serious things — it is full attention that happens to be pleasurable.

Week 40

Spring Flood / Overwhelm

may ariseoverwhelm, as more than the channel can carry

companionthe spring flood

The spring flood is not the river's failure; it is a snowpack larger than the banks were built for. What it reframes: overwhelm is not a sign something is wrong with you — it is information about capacity and the conditions that shaped it.

Constellation Five — Stillness and Its Difficulty

Emotions that resist motion, or that motion cannot reach.

Week 41

Desert / Boredom

may ariseboredom, as the waiting before what doesn't yet know it will happen

companionthe desert

The desert is not empty but extraordinarily patient, holding everything it needs for when the rain comes. What it reframes: boredom is not a failure of engagement — it is the waiting that makes germination possible.

Week 42

Turtle / Patience

may arisepatience, as a different relationship to time

companionthe turtle

The turtle does not wait so much as inhabit time differently; its pace is a method, not a concession. What it reframes: patience is not endurance — it is a changed relationship to when things are allowed to happen.

Week 43

Estuary / Ambivalence

may ariseambivalence, as holding two true things at once

companionthe estuary

The estuary is neither river nor sea but both continuously, with a chemistry neither could produce alone. What it reframes: ambivalence is not weakness of character — it is ecological richness.

Constellation Six — Transition States

Emotions that mark passage — between states, between selves.

Week 44

Fog / Confusion

may ariseconfusion, as the mind midway through changing form

companionfog

Fog is water that is neither rain nor air. What it reframes: confusion is not ignorance — it is what thinking looks like while it is transforming.

Week 45

Chrysalis / Uncertainty

may ariseuncertainty, as the condition in which the future is built

companionthe chrysalis

The chrysalis holds dissolved material that has not yet become anything; the transformation is improvised, not planned. What it reframes: uncertainty is not waiting for clarity — it is what becoming looks like from the inside.

Constellation Seven — Anger and Its Intelligence

Emotions that are often refused, and that carry information when they are received.

Week 46

Storm / Anger

may ariseanger, as the redistribution of what was held too long

companionthe storm

The storm moves energy from high pressure to low, does its work, and dissipates; it does not choose its targets. What it reframes: anger is not a loss of control — it is information about what has been accumulating, and where.

Week 47

Wasp / Irritability

may ariseirritability, as a boundary crossed too many times

companionthe wasp

The wasp stings when the nest is threatened and the warning has gone unheeded. What it reframes: irritability is not a character flaw — it is a threshold, exceeded, speaking.

Constellation Eight — Belonging and Its Limits

The emotional territory of being among others, and the cost of it.

Week 48

Wolf Pack / Longing

may ariselonging, as the shape of what you know is possible

companionthe wolf pack

The wolf howls not to mourn but to locate the pack; the sound is a way of knowing others exist. What it reframes: longing is not suffering from absence — it is proof of a capacity for connection.

Movement Four · Weeks 49–52

Synthesis

The year turns outward and does not close. The feelings here are the feelings of looking back, and of not arriving.

Week 49

The Public Record of Learning

may arisethe recognition of seeing what you couldn't see from inside

companionthe coral record

Coral keeps its years as visible rings; the archive is the body itself. What it knows: looking back at early work shows you what was true while you made it and invisible to you then.

Week 50

Addressing the Evidence Gap

may arisethe frustration of being real and unmeasured

companionbioluminescence

Bioluminescence is light below the depth our instruments reach — not absent, only unmeasured. What it knows: something can happen that no questionnaire catches, and the failure to measure it is not the same as its not being there.

Week 51

Scaling Creative Health

may arisethe humbling of a problem larger than one practice

companionthe mycorrhizal network at forest scale

A forest's underground network has no centre and cannot be installed quickly; it was built over centuries. What it knows: some infrastructure outlasts its builders, and the work is to add to it, not to finish it.

Week 52

Arriving at the Horizon

may arisethe honest disappointment, and the quiet freedom, of arriving

companionthe horizon

The horizon is not a destination but the edge of what can currently be seen, which moves exactly as far as you travel toward it. What it knows: you do not arrive; you learn to see from further out, which is a different and harder thing than finishing.
