# Make Space Weekly Post Template — the emotion-first shape

## The template that produced Weeks 1 and 2 — written down so it can be drafted from, not rediscovered

*This is the operational shape. It is reverse-engineered from the two finished pieces that work — "What the dark is for" (Week 1) and "Already, partly, here" (Week 2). Both run the identical eight moves below. Draft new weeks into this.*

*How it relates to the other docs: the six commitments are the law; this is the form that enacts them. Where this template and the running order in `MakeSpace_Weekly_Template_Revised.md` disagree, this one wins — that older file describes an earlier concept-first order and should be reconciled to this. The `MakeSpace_Orientation_Essay_Form.md` is the separate shape for standalone framing essays; the commitments below cover it too.*

---

## The commitments (the law — these govern every move below)

1. **Feel before think.** The reader meets a feeling in the body before any concept. This is the most important thing the writing does.
2. **Stay inside the feeling — never explain it.** Never step outside the emotion to point at your own method. Never tell a participant what is happening inside them.
3. **The natural body is a companion; the feeling is named in pencil.** Offered as *may arise*, never as ink. The reader may find a different feeling, or none.
4. **Never diagnose the reader.** No clinical label, no verdict. Grammar of possibility — *may, might, could*.
5. **Center HPSM voices and ancestral knowledge — with complete respect.** Centered, not appended. Named precisely, in their own words, attributed to source, within what is yours to share.
6. **The truth behind the facts.** The facts are the path, not the destination. Precision with the facts; deep respect for the reader.

*If any move below ever pulls against a commitment, the commitment wins.*

## The eight moves

### Move 1 — The feeling, met in the body
Open inside a felt, sensory experience of the emotion this week may bring. No concept, no practitioner, no abstraction, no meta. A door in the body.

- **In pencil.** Offer the feeling; do not assert it. The reader may find it, or find something else. *Week 1:* "Maybe you know the particular quiet of a page that is still blank." *Week 2:* "Maybe you know the feeling of being somewhere without quite being in it yet."
- **Close the opening by handing the feeling back.** End the section by returning the feeling to the reader as theirs. *Both weeks:* "…and sometimes nothing so clear… Whatever is or isn't there, it is yours, and you don't have to name it to begin."
- **Fresh frame every week — do not clone.** Do not reuse a prior week's opening device (the hovering hand, "there is a particular moment," the "not a sign you did something wrong" beat). Find the sensory doorway this week's own feeling demands.
- Length: 2–3 short paragraphs.

### Move 2 — The turn to the companion
One sentence pivots from the human feeling to the natural body; then the body is described as itself.

- **The one-line turn.** *Week 1:* "This is not only a human difficulty. Something underground meets it too, more completely than we ever do." *Week 2:* "You are not the only body that arrives before it decides to. Consider the morning tide."
- **The body as itself — accurate, no metaphor.** Real biology or physics, specific enough to picture and feel. (The seed's stored reserves, the apical hook; the moon's pull, the tidal bulge.) Get it right; it carries `[VERIFY]` in the anchors.
- **Let it be.** End with permission to let the body simply be before it means anything. *Both weeks:* "There's no need to make it mean anything yet. Let it be…"
- Length: one short paragraph of biology + the let-it-be line.

### Move 3 — The idea arrives as company
Only now does the concept enter — as something that *helped*, not as a thesis. The practitioner follows.

- **The soft bridge back.** *Both weeks:* "It is, perhaps, the same [dark / arriving] some of us meet…"
- **The idea as company, not authority.** *Both weeks:* "And it has helped me, [in that held breath / on those mornings], to sit with something [Winnicott / Gendlin] spent his life noticing…" The key is a door the reader already half-feels, from a thinker outside the field.
- **The practitioner, after the feeling.** In the middle of specific, verifiable work ([Milner] with a brush; [van der Kolk] in the clinic). Begin where the practice genuinely comes from (commitment 5). No invented sessions or quotes.
- Length: 2–3 short paragraphs.

### Move 4 — The framework and the chorus
Research arrives late, in service of what's been felt — and honest about its own limits.

- **Honesty inside the sentence.** State the limit in the same breath as the claim. *Both weeks:* the broad evidence is real, but the specific thing this week leans on "is not something the studies cleanly isolate… it lives, for now, in practice and theory, and the honest version says so."
- **The chorus, in genuine conversation.** Voices across field and century speaking to each other, not cited in sequence (Milner ↔ Winnicott ↔ Csikszentmihalyi; Gendlin ↔ van der Kolk ↔ Levine).
- **An HPSM voice, load-bearing through the pillar.** Not appended — the reason the week sits under its pillar. *Week 1:* bell hooks and the right to create (Creative Access). *Week 2:* tayla shanaye and whose body is given the conditions to arrive (Body as Site).
- **Seat a real doubt — and let the body answer it.** Give a genuine counter-voice full weight, then let the natural body answer gently. *Week 1:* "the seed is not freestyling… it keeps the direction and lets the picture go." *Week 2:* "the tide is drawn, not forced… you do not have to feel fully arrived to begin."
- Length: 3–4 short paragraphs.

### Move 5 — Into the studio
First-person, from a peer working alongside the reader — never an instructor.

- **The "I" alongside, in the same condition.** *Week 1:* "I won't know what I'm doing either; that isn't modesty, it's the actual condition we are both in." *Week 2:* "I won't know what I'm doing either."
- **Release from obligation.** "You don't have to do what I'm doing. Only the smallest reach that is still yours."
- **The check-in.** Weight, breath, one thing the body is carrying — not to fix it, only to know it is there. If the week's practice is arrival itself (as in Week 2), the check-in *is* the whole studio; make nothing.
- **A way out.** If inward attention becomes too much, name a gentle exit (eyes open, rest on something ordinary). Never a command.

### Move 6 — Watch · Share · Rest
- **Watch:** one line on what the session opens, then the link. A standalone moving-practice session.
- **Share:** one line on why this territory is easier in company, then a single concrete prompt grounded in the week's body.
- **Rest:** a genuine open question for the field — not a summary. Close: "The field is still working this out. So are we."

### Move 7 — Next week
One line naming next week's companion and territory — enough to anticipate, not enough to give away. One week at a time; a feeling not yet reached is not yet the reader's to hold.

### Move 8 — Anchors
Every empirical or biographical claim carries `[VERIFY]` until checked against the source (not memory, not an AI draft — character by character). Include: the natural body's biology; the key; the practitioner; the framework source with its stated limitation; the chorus (with the HPSM voice); an **ancestral/traditional knowledge** line attributed to its source community (or left out if none can be properly attributed); and the **counter-voice** source. A missing citation is more honest than a wrong one.

## What breaks it (the negative rules)
- **The meta-reveal.** Turning to say "notice what I just did to you." This is the callous move that made the first Week 1 fail. Never narrate the technique back to the reader.
- **Diagnosing.** Telling the reader what they feel or what is wrong with them. Stay in the grammar of possibility.
- **Front-loading the concept.** Opening on the thinker or the framework instead of the feeling.
- **Cloning last week.** Reusing a prior opening frame. Each week finds its own doorway.
- **The performing "I".** The clever essayist showing off, instead of the maker in the same dark.

## Pre-draft and pre-publish checklist
- [ ] Does the reader feel something in the body before any concept? (The one box that matters most.)
- [ ] Does the piece stay inside the feeling — no meta-reveal, nothing narrated about the reader's interior?
- [ ] Is the feeling in pencil, nothing diagnosed?
- [ ] Is the natural body accurate and described as itself, then let be?
- [ ] Does the idea arrive as company, after the feeling — not as the opener?
- [ ] Is an HPSM voice load-bearing through the pillar, and any ancestral knowledge attributed to its source?
- [ ] Is a real doubt seated and answered by the body?
- [ ] Is the studio "I" alongside, with release from obligation and a way out?
- [ ] Does Rest leave a genuine open question?
- [ ] Is the opening frame new — not a reuse of a prior week?
- [ ] Does every claim carry a source or a `[VERIFY]`?
